India's History | Reflect

India's history is an ancient and complex tapestry that unfolds over thousands of years, encompassing remarkable civilizations, diverse cultures, and profound spiritual traditions. Situated in South Asia, the Indian subcontinent has been a cradle of human civilization since prehistoric times. The story of India's history is one of profound societal, cultural, and political developments, shaped by empires, invasions, trade routes, and the assimilation of diverse peoples.

India's history is a vast and intricate mosaic that continues to evolve, reflecting the interplay of countless civilizations, religions, languages, and traditions. It is a land where ancient wisdom meets modern aspirations, making it a fascinating subject of study and exploration.

India's Progress | Renew

After gaining independence from British colonial rule in 1947, India embarked on a journey of development and growth that has transformed the nation in various spheres. The post-independence period has witnessed significant progress in areas such as economy, infrastructure, education, science and technology, and social welfare.

India's development and growth after independence have transformed it into one of the world's largest economies and a key player on the global stage. The nation's journey of progress and its aspiration to achieve inclusive and sustainable development continue to shape its future trajectory.

India leading the G-20 Summit | Radiate

G20 is a major international grouping that brings together 19 of the world's major economies and the European Union, with its members accounting for more than 80% of global GDP, 75% of global trade and 60% of global population. Prime Minister Narendra Modi has been leading India's representation at G20 Summits since 2014. India has been a member of the G20 since its inception in 1999. India will be holding the G20 Presidency from 1 December 2022 and will convene the G20 Leaders' Summit in 2023 for the first time.

During its term, India will hold more than 200 meetings across some 50 cities involving ministers, officials and civil society, leading up to a marquee summit in the capital New Delhi in September 2023. The summit will be attended by around 30 heads of state and government, from G20 members and invited countries.
